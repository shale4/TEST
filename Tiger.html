Tiger

/Panthera tigris/

About the tiger

The tiger is a carnivorous animal and the largest cat species in the world. Tigers live in rainforests and grasslands throughout many Asian countries and parts of eastern Russia. Most tiger species eat mainly deer, pigs, buffalos, and antelope. Many species of tigers are currently at risk due to climate change as tigers are endangered with there only being around 3,900 tigers left in the wild with this number constantly declining.

How the tiger is affected by climate change

Many species of tigers such as Bengal tigers are estimated to be wiped out due to climate change. This is due to rising temperatures and rising sea levels that could destroy tigers and their habitats. The rising temperatures caused by global warming also causes many animals that tigers prey on to flee from forests in which tigers live in causing tigers to have little food to survive.
