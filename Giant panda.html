Giant Panda

/Ailuropoda melanoleuca/

About giant pandas

Giant pandas are a species of bear native to southern-central China. The giant panda is an omnivore and spends up to 10-16 hours a day eating bamboo as it does not provide many nutrients for the panda. Pandas often live in trees but are often always on the ground due to them needing to eat. Giant pandas can live up to 30 years old and can weigh up to 135kg.

How giant pandas are affected by climate change

Since bamboo is about 90% of a giant panda's diet, they rely on it to survive. However, due to climate change, some of the bamboo plants that pandas consume may die out. Giant pandas are also at risk due to the heat of global warming as they experience heat stress at 25 degrees and above. Due to the heat, pandas are forced to higher elevations to survive. However, the bamboo will most likely not be able to grow far up in habitats causing the giant pandas to be left without food and would then starve.
