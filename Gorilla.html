Mountain Gorilla

/Gorilla beringei beringei/

About mountain gorillas

Mountain gorillas are a species of gorilla that lives high in the Virunga mountains, a group of volcanoes that border the Democratic Republic of Congo, Rwanda and Uganda. There is estimated to be only around 1,000 living in the wild currently and the species has been labelled as endangered. Mountain gorillas primarily eat roots, fruit, and tree bark. Mountain gorillas are one of the most powerful living primates but they are near the bottom of the food chain and don't have many predators.

How mountain gorillas are affected by climate change

Changes in rainfall patterns and temperatures can affect the gorillas' food supply as they mainly eat vegetables and fruits, cause heat stress, increase the chance that they lose habitat to forest fires, and cause new diseases to form for which the gorillas have little or no immunity to. Mountain gorillas play a very important role in the environment as, without them, the food chain would be disrupted.
