Polar ice caps

/North/South poles/

About the polar ice caps

The polar ice caps are large areas of ice at the north and south poles of the planet. The polar ice caps are believed to be around 3-15 million years old. The polar ice caps help cool down the polar regions of the world and help contribute to moderating climate change. The polar ice caps cover around 15 million square kilometres of the earth and contain around 25-30 million cubic kilometres of ice.

How the polar ice caps are affected by climate change

Since the polar ice caps are made of ice, they are very vulnerable to climate change and rising temperatures. The polar ice caps have been melting at a rate of around 13% every decade and have led to the amount of arctic ice decline by almost 95% in only 30 years. It is also predicted that the artic may be ice-free in 2040 due to the rapid speed of global warming. The melting of the ice caps has many negative impacts on the rest of the world such as releasing frozen greenhouse gasses trapped in the ice, impacting global temperatures, rising sea levels, and harming many species of animals that live in the north and south pole.
