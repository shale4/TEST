Polar Bear

/Ursus maritimus/

About polar bears

The polar bear is a carnivorous species of bear that lives in the arctic. They live on the ice chunks of the polar ice caps and eat seals and arctic foxes. Polar bears only live for around 20-25 years though they can grow up to 250kg. Polar bears are at the top of the food chain and help keep balance so that the artic ecosystem can thrive. Polar bears are also the largest carnivorous land mammals on earth.

How polar bears are affected by climate change

Since polar bears live on the ice caps of the arctic, they are very vulnerable to climate change and the melting of the polar ice caps. The ice from the artic is rapidly melting away so eventually many polar bears will not have a home anymore. Another threat to the polar bears caused by the melting of the icecaps is that many of the seals will lose the ice needed to raise their young meaning the polar bears will not have any food to eat.
