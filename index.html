Climate Defence

What is climate change?

Climate change is the long term change in global temperature and weather patterns caused by human impact. The effects of climate change can vary from as much as more rainy days to many more major hurricanes and tornadoes that wipe out parts of countries. Climate change can cause effects all around the world such as higher temperatures, drought, higher sea levels, more severe storms, habitats and environments to be destroyed, and species to become extinct.

Why does climate change matter?

Climate change matters as it threatens the existence of humankind and the planet we live on. Even though our generation may not see the full consequences and effects of climate change, that doesn't mean future generations won't. Studying and analysing climate change is a key step to understanding and predicting how climate change will increase and change the future. Not only does climate change affect environments and animals, but it also affects our food sources and farmers growing the food. This is why climate change must be taken more seriously and taken action against.

The Alps

The alps are the highest mountain range in all of Europe located across countries such as France, Italy, and Austria. The Alps' highest peak is as high as 15,776 feet high. The Alps is home to many species of animals such as goats, owls, and cats.

Bees

Bees are very common winged insects that live all around the world. Bees play a key part in pollination and honey production which makes them a very important species of insect. Bees live in large groups and form hives in which they produce honey and protect a queen bee.

Polar Ice Caps

The polar ice caps are large areas of ice at the north and south poles of the planet. The polar ice caps are believd to be around 3-15 million years old. The polar ice caps help cool down the polar regions of the world and help contribute to moderating climate change.

“The world is reaching the tipping point beyond which climate change may become irreversible. If this happens, we risk denying present and future generations the right to a healthy and sustainable planet – the whole of humanity stands to lose.”
- Kofi Annan, Former Secretary-General of UN
