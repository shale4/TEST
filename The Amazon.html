Amazon Rainforest

/Brazil/

About the amazon rainforest

The Amazon rainforest is a very large rainforest in northwestern brazil. The Amazon rainforest covers 1.7 million square miles of land and is home to many different species of birds, monkeys, and insects and is also has many species of plants and trees. The Amazon rainforest is home to the amazon river, the largest river in the world which is around 6,500 km long.

How the amazon rainforest is affected by climate change

The Amazon rainforest is severely at risk due to climate change. This is because of many factors such as forest degradation, drying up, and extreme weather. As the planet is heating up due to climate change, the Amazon rainforest is also slowly drying up. Once the Amazon becomes too dry and degraded, the rainforest won't be able to generate its rain anymore meaning the ecosystem will collapse and most animal and plant species will die out.
